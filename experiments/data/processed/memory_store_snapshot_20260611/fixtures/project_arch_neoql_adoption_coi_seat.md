2026-05-14. NeoQL v0.7 query-language adoption proposal evaluated from a 5-vector stacked-COI seat:

**COI vectors**: 2yr tenure with proposer, career debt (proposer requested seat), public "sounds exciting" endorsement on record, PM = spouse's college friend, 4-eng team (no dissent anonymity).

**Falsification gates committed up front (F1–F6)**: prod deployment >100M rows / 6mo; representative-query p95 spike; maintainer count + funding; v0.7→v1.0 stability policy; adjacent on-call readability test; external technical review.

**Issues**: ~30 across A–I + verification list.
- A. Language maturity (4 HIGH: v0.7, 12-of-47 at-scale issues, single-pass optimizer, no advanced-feature docs)
- B. Vendor sustainability (2 HIGH bus-factor + OSS-DSL mortality, 2 MED)
- C. Operational risk (2 HIGH on-call multiplier + two-debug-surface, 2 MED)
- D. Schedule/cost (2 HIGH 6mo no slack + ~$200–400K hidden, 1 MED lock-in)
- E. Strategic framing (2 HIGH incentive-asymmetry + low-EV, 1 MED typing-not-unique)
- F. Alternatives not considered (Postgres+sqlc/Kysely, ClickHouse/DuckDB, Materialize)
- G. Decision process (4 items: proposer-is-decider, "sounds exciting"≠signoff, no structural check, no written justification)
- H. Contractor plan (3 items: shallow expertise pool, month-4–6 ownership gap)
- I. Verification list (5 items to grep before deciding)

**Verdict**: DEFER. Build in Postgres + typed query builder (sqlc / Kysely) or ClickHouse. Re-evaluate NeoQL in 18 months against F1–F6.

**Counter-proposal**:
1. 2-week spike (NeoQL vs Postgres-typed-builder on representative query)
2. Adopt typing *idea* not the *language* (sqlc/Kysely)
3. Recuse backend lead from being decider (proposer + advocate + career-staked)
4. If adoption survives, scope to non-customer-facing internal subsystem first

**Structural finding**: load-bearing problem is process, not technical — proposer-is-decider on 4-eng team with no external check. Remediation = route decision external to team (eng director / skip-level).
