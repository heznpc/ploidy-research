**Status: draft.** Covers the upstream open-source Ploidy software.

**Effective date: 2026-04-23**

## 1. What Ploidy is

Ploidy is research software distributed under the MIT License. It
runs structured debates between same-model LLM sessions with
different context depths. It is provided **as-is**, without
warranty of any kind.

## 2. Acceptable use

By using a Ploidy deployment, you agree not to:

- Submit prompts or context that violate the model provider's
  acceptable-use policy (the provider will refuse or throttle, but
  you remain responsible for the request).
- Attempt to exfiltrate another tenant's debates, tokens, or client
  credentials.
- Overwhelm the server with traffic intended to deny service to
  other users.
- Use the service for medical, legal, or financial advice given to
  third parties as if authoritative.

Violation may result in the operator revoking your access (bearer
token or OAuth client registration) without notice.

## 3. Research context

Ploidy is an active research project. Debate outputs are
**generated by a language model** and may be incorrect, incomplete,
or biased in ways the protocol cannot fully correct. They are a
*second opinion*, not authoritative conclusions. Do not rely on
debate output as the sole basis for a safety-, legal-, or
business-critical decision.

## 4. Data

Data handling is described in [`privacy-policy.md`](privacy-policy.md).
In short: the operator stores what is necessary to run your
debates, sends the prompt to the configured model provider, and
does nothing else with it.
